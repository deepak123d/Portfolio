UX Design App Case Study

The product:The food menu app I designed is a user-friendly platform tailored for patrons of my favorite hometown restaurant, "Taste of Home."

Project duration:February 2024 to May 2024

Problem:Ordering food at restaurants faces several challenges, both in-person and for takeout. The lack of a dedicated mobile application for menu browsing and ordering often leads to a frustrating experience. To make ordering easier for users, I designed an app.

Goal:This portfolio's goal is to demonstrate my UX design skills by creating a mobile app for Ghalley's Restaurant that improves menu accessibility, streamlines the ordering process, and enhances customer engagement and satisfaction.

My role:In this project, my role was as the lead UX designer, responsible for conceptualizing, designing, and refining the user interface and experience of the food menu app for "Taste of Home."

The project goal:The goal of the project was to create a user-friendly food menu app that would seamlessly connect patrons with the offerings of "Taste of Home," facilitating easy ordering, table reservations, and enhancing the overall dining experience.

Target Audience:

Regular diners: Regular diners at Ghalley's Restaurant who want a convenient way to browse the menu and place orders.

Busy individuals: Individuals with tight schedules looking for a quick and efficient way to order takeout or delivery.

Parents and guardians: Parents and guardians seeking an easy method to customize orders for their family's dietary needs and preferences.

Key Challenges or Constraints:

  • Menu Complexity: Ensuring the app can handle a diverse menu with multiple categories, customization options, and special dietary requirements.
  • User Experience: Designing an intuitive and user-friendly interface that caters to a wide range of users, including those who are not tech-savvy.
  • Order Customization: Allowing users to easily customize their orders with specific dietary preferences and special instructions without causing confusion or errors.

User Research

Initial Design Concepts

Some initial design concepts for Ghalley's Restaurant are:

  1. Homepage and Navigation: Clean, intuitive layout with easy navigation. Prominent search bar for quick menu item lookup and categorized menu sections.
  2. Menu Browsing: Visual and interactive menu browsing experience. High-quality images of dishes with detailed descriptions and filters for dietary preferences.
  3. Order Customization: Flexible and user-friendly customization options. Customizable ingredients and portion sizes and special instructions field for specific preferences.
  4. Feedback and Support: Easy access to customer support and feedback. Post-order feedback form to rate the experience and direct chat support with the restaurant.

Conclusion

What was learned: Throughout the design process, several key insights were gained. Understanding user needs through research and usability studies highlighted the importance of intuitive navigation and clear visual design. Iterative testing and feedback were crucial in refining the app’s functionality and ensuring it met user expectations. Additionally, the significance of accessibility and customization options became evident, driving design decisions that improved user satisfaction.

Next steps: Moving forward, the focus will be on implementing the high-fidelity prototype into a fully functional app. This will involve collaborating with developers to ensure the design translates seamlessly into a real-world application. Continuous user testing will be conducted to gather feedback and make iterative improvements. Future enhancements may include integrating loyalty programs, personalized recommendations, and advanced order tracking features to further elevate the user experience and engagement.
