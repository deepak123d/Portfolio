UX Design Responsive Website Case Study

The product:A responsive website for Ghalley Restaurant that showcases the menu, allows for online reservations and ordering, and highlights the restaurant's unique ambiance and story.

Project duration:February 2024 to May 2024

Problem:Ghalley Restaurant lacks an online presence, making it difficult for potential customers to discover its offerings, make reservations, or order food online.

Goal:To design and develop an engaging and user-friendly website that effectively promotes Ghalley Restaurant, facilitates online reservations and orders, and enhances customer engagement.

My role:As the lead designer and developer, I am responsible for conducting research, creating the design, developing the website, and ensuring a seamless user experience across all devices.

The project goal:To create a visually appealing and functional website that increases customer engagement, improves online visibility, and boosts sales through online reservations and orders.

Target Audience:

Local residents, tourists visiting the hometown, and food enthusiasts looking for authentic and unique dining experiences.

Key Challenges or Constraints:

  • Ensuring the website is fully responsive and accessible on all devices.
  • Integrating a reliable and user-friendly online reservation and ordering system.
  • Maintaining the authenticity and unique charm of Ghalley Restaurant through design and content.

User Research

Initial Design Concepts

Initial Design Concepts for Ghalley Restaurant Website:

  1. Home Page: A high-quality, full-width image of a popular dish or the restaurant's inviting ambiance with a welcoming tagline.
  2. Menu Page: Sections for appetizers, main courses, desserts, and beverages. Each menu item includes a detailed description, price, and high-quality image.
  3. Reservations Page: Simple form to book a table with fields for date, time, number of guests, and special requests. Interactive calendar to check availability and book directly.
  4. Order Online Page: User-friendly interface to select menu items, customize orders, and add to cart.
  5. About Us Page: A brief history of Ghalley Restaurant, its mission, and core values. Photos and bios of key staff members and chefs.

Low-Fidelity Prototype

Low-Fidelity Prototype: Interactive version of digital wireframes, focusing on usability and basic functionality without detailed design.

Usability Study

Usability Study: A usability study tests the website with real users to assess ease of use, navigation, and overall experience. Participants perform tasks while observers note any difficulties, providing insights to improve the design and better meet user needs.

High-Fidelity Prototype

High-Fidelity Prototype: Detailed, polished version resembling the final product, including colors, typography, images, and interactive features.

Accessibility Considerations

Accessibility Considerations for the Ghalley Restaurant Website:

  1. Keyboard Navigation: Ensure all interactive elements (menus, buttons, forms) are accessible via keyboard, with clear focus indicators for navigation without a mouse.
  2. Alt Text for Images: Provide descriptive alt text for all images, especially for menu items and promotions, to aid visually impaired users using screen readers.
  3. Color Contrast: Use high-contrast color schemes to ensure text readability, avoiding problematic color combinations to assist users with visual impairments, including color blindness.

Conclusion

Impact: The new Ghalley Restaurant website will enhance online presence, improve customer convenience with detailed menus and easy reservations, and increase orders. It will attract new customers, boost satisfaction and loyalty, and strengthen the restaurant's brand image, driving business growth.

What was learned: Throughout the design process for the Ghalley Restaurant website, I gained valuable insights into the importance of prioritizing user needs and preferences. By conducting thorough user research, including surveys, interviews, and competitor analysis, I learned to identify pain points and design solutions that address them effectively. Understanding the significance of detailed menu information, convenient online booking systems, and a seamless mobile experience has highlighted the key areas where the website can make the most impact on enhancing the overall user experience.

Next steps: Moving forward, the next steps for the Ghalley Restaurant website project involve several key actions. Firstly, prototype testing will be conducted to gather feedback and refine the design before launch. Following this, content creation will focus on developing engaging and informative text and visuals to enrich the website's storytelling. Technical development will then commence to implement the design using a suitable technology stack. Finally, the website will be launched and promoted to drive traffic, with continuous improvement based on user feedback and analytics ensuring that the platform evolves to meet the changing needs of its audience over time.
